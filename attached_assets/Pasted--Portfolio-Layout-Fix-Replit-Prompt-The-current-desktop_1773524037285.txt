# Portfolio Layout Fix — Replit Prompt

The current desktop portfolio layout is stacking cards behind each other in depth (front to back), so you only see thin edges peeking out. This is wrong. I need a HORIZONTAL fan layout, not a depth stack.

## Exactly What I Want (reference the right-side screenshot)

Imagine 5 cards laid out on a table, overlapping horizontally like a hand of playing cards fanned out left to right. All cards are visible — not hidden behind one front card. Here's the specific layout:

### Card Arrangement
- All 5 cards are in a HORIZONTAL ROW, overlapping each other by about 60-70% of their width
- Cards are arranged left to right, each one offset maybe 80-100px to the right of the previous one
- The cards tilt/rotate very slightly — leftmost cards tilted maybe -2 degrees, rightmost tilted +2 degrees, creating a subtle fan arc
- Each card is the SAME SIZE — roughly 500px wide and 350px tall
- Every card's browser chrome (dots + URL bar) is visible at the top
- Every card shows its content (screenshot or placeholder)
- You can see meaningful portions of ALL 5 cards at once, not just edges

### Which Card Is "Active"
- One card is considered active/highlighted — it sits slightly higher (translateY -10px) and has a stronger shadow
- The active card is also slightly in front (higher z-index) of its neighbors
- On hover, a card becomes active — it lifts up slightly and its neighbors shift apart a bit to give it room
- The transition between states is smooth, 300-400ms ease-out

### Key Difference From Current Implementation
RIGHT NOW: One big card in front, others hidden behind it as thin slivers → THIS IS WRONG
WHAT I WANT: All cards visible in a horizontal fan, overlapping but each one clearly showing its content → LIKE A HAND OF PLAYING CARDS
