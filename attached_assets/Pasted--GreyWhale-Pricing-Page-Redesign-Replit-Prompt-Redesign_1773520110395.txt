# GreyWhale Pricing Page Redesign — Replit Prompt

Redesign the pricing section on the /pricing page. Three tiers instead of two. The goal is subtle upsell psychology — the $149 tier should feel slightly incomplete, nudging people toward $249 without feeling manipulative. Every tier should feel like a good deal, but the middle tier should feel like the obvious choice.

## Pricing Tiers

### Tier 1 — Starter · $149/month
Position this as "everything you need to get online" — solid, respectable, but clearly the baseline.

Includes:
- Custom designed website
- Up to 5 pages
- Mobile responsive
- Basic SEO (meta tags, sitemap)
- Hosting & SSL included
- Contact form
- 1 round of revisions per month
- 48-hour support response time

What it does NOT include (and this is the nudge): no analytics, no booking/scheduling integration, no Google Business Profile optimization, no priority support. These are things a business owner will want once they see the tier above.

### Tier 2 — Professional · $249/month · ← Mark this as "Most popular"
This is the anchor. It should feel like the obvious choice — everything in Starter plus the things that actually help a business grow.

Includes:
- Everything in Starter
- Up to 10 pages
- Google Analytics setup
- Google Business Profile optimization
- Booking or scheduling integration
- Contact forms with lead notifications
- Advanced SEO (local schema, FAQ markup, structured data)
- 2 rounds of revisions per month
- 24-hour priority support response time

The jump from Starter to Professional should feel like: "for $100 more I get analytics, booking, better SEO, and faster support — that's a no-brainer."

### Tier 3 — Business · $349/month
Position this as the premium option for businesses that are serious about growth. It exists partly to make Professional look like a deal by comparison (price anchoring), but it should also be genuinely valuable for the right client.

Includes:
- Everything in Professional
- Up to 20 pages
- E-commerce functionality (product pages, cart, checkout)
- Custom integrations (API connections, third-party tools)
- Blog setup and management
- Monthly performance report
- Unlimited revisions
- Same-day priority support

## Design Notes

### Card Layout
Three cards side by side, centered. The Professional card (middle) should be visually emphasized:
- Slightly taller or elevated compared to the other two
- A "Most popular" badge at the top
- A subtle highlighted border (e.g. 2px black border vs 1px light gray on the others)
- The CTA button on Professional should be solid black with white text; the other two should be outlined/ghost buttons

### Visual Hierarchy
- Tier name: small, uppercase, muted text at top of card
- Price: large, bold, dominant — "$249" should be the first thing your eye lands on
- "/month" in smaller, lighter text beside the price
- "No upfront cost" in muted text below the price
- Feature list: clean, no bullet points — use small checkmarks (✓) in a muted color, with feature text in regular weight
- Features that are unique to this tier (not in the tier below) should be in slightly bolder text or have a subtle "New" or "+" indicator so people can quickly see what they gain by upgrading

### The Subtle Nudge
Below the Starter card's feature list, add a small muted line: "Want analytics and booking? → See Professional"
Below the Professional card's feature list, add: "Need e-commerce? → See Business"
These should be subtle — small text, muted color, feels like a helpful suggestion not a sales push.

### No CMS Language
Do NOT mention "CMS access" or "CMS" anywhere on the page. Instead, describe the self-service editing capability as: "Edit your own text and images anytime" — this is clearer to a non-technical business owner and doesn't commit to a specific technical implementation.

## FAQ Updates

Update the FAQ to reflect the three-tier structure:

**"Which plan is right for me?"**
Starter is perfect if you just need a clean, professional website to establish your online presence. Professional is our most popular plan — it adds analytics, booking integration, and advanced SEO so your site actually brings in new customers. Business is for companies that need e-commerce, custom integrations, or a larger web presence.

**"Can I upgrade later?"**
Yes. You can move up to a higher plan anytime. We'll add the new features to your existing site — no rebuild required.

**"Is there a contract?"**
No. All plans are month-to-month. Cancel anytime.

**"What does 'rounds of revisions' mean?"**
A revision is any change you want us to make that goes beyond basic text and image edits — things like layout changes, new sections, adding a page, or design tweaks. Starter includes 1 per month, Professional includes 2, and Business includes unlimited.

**"Do I own my website?"**
Yes. Your site is built on code we deliver to you. If you ever leave, you take everything with you.
