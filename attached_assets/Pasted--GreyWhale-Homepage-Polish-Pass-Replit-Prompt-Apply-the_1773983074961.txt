## 3. TERMINAL ANIMATION — LIGHT MODE, OVERLAPPING TABS

The black terminal window on the right side of the hero needs three changes:

**A. Switch to light mode.** White/light gray background, dark text. The current dark terminal looks flat against the white page. A light terminal with subtle gray chrome will feel more integrated and modern.

**B. Add overlapping windows.** Show TWO terminal/code windows slightly overlapping — like multiple browser tabs or app windows open on a desktop. The front window has the typing animation. The back window (peeking out behind, offset maybe 20px right and 20px up) shows a static snippet — maybe some HTML/CSS code or a completed deployment log. This creates depth and visual interest.

**C. Keep the typing animation** but make sure the text colors work on a light background. Green checkmarks can stay green. Command text in dark gray/black. URLs and technical terms in a subtle blue or teal.

## 6. IMPROVE LANGUAGE THROUGHOUT — SELL, DON'T DESCRIBE

Go through EVERY section and tighten the copy. The rule: if it reads like a brochure, rewrite it to read like a pitch. People scan, they don't read. Every line needs to either create urgency, build desire, or remove an objection.

Specific rewrites:

**Visibility section subheadline:**
CURRENT: "Most agencies hand you a pretty site and call it a day. We build you a machine that climbs Google rankings, captures local searches, and sends real customers to your door. Design is the surface. Visibility is the engine."
BETTER: "Other agencies hand you a pretty site and disappear. We build a machine that climbs Google, captures searches, and sends customers through your door."
(Shorter. Punchier. Cut the last line — it's explaining something the reader already gets.)

**Local SEO section:**
CURRENT: "Every site we build includes structured data markup, local schema, Google Business Profile optimization, meta tags targeting your city and services, XML sitemaps, and mobile-first indexing. This isn't an add-on. It's the foundation."
BETTER: "Structured data. Local schema. Google Business Profile optimization. City-targeted meta tags. XML sitemaps. Mobile-first indexing. All standard. All included. All built to get you found."
(Punchy list format. Reads faster. Hits harder.)

**Mesh network section:**
CURRENT text is good but tighten it:
BETTER: "Every GreyWhale client is part of something bigger. We connect your site to a growing network of real local business websites — all linking to each other behind the scenes. Every new client makes YOUR rankings stronger. Your SEO doesn't just start strong. It compounds."
(Cut "Invisible to your visitors. Devastating for your competition." — it's trying too hard.)

**One-time build trap closing line:**
CURRENT: "One-time builds decay. GreyWhale sites compound. That's why you pay monthly — because your site should get better every month, not worse."
BETTER: "One-time builds decay. GreyWhale sites compound."
(Kill the explanation. The two sentences say it all. Let them land.)

## 7. FIRST TWO SECTIONS ARE EVERYTHING

Most visitors will decide based on the hero and the visibility section alone. These two sections need to be FLAWLESS.

The hero should feel like a punch — bold headline, clear value, immediate CTA, impressive terminal animation. No wasted words.

The visibility section should feel like a wake-up call — "you're invisible on Google and losing money every day." The stats should animate in with impact (count up from 0 to the final number as they enter viewport, not just appear statically).

Everything after these two sections is supporting evidence. If the first two don't hook them, nothing else matters.
