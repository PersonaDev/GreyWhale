## OVERALL DESIGN DIRECTION

- Subminimal. White space is the dominant design element.
- Black and white with minimal accent. No colors competing for attention.
- Premium micro-animations and scroll-triggered transitions throughout — elements fading in, sliding up, parallax effects, smooth reveals. The animations should feel intentional and weighted, not bouncy or playful. Think luxury brand, not startup.
- Typography-driven. Large, bold headlines do the heavy lifting. No icons, no illustrations, no stock photos.
- Every section transitions smoothly into the next. The page should feel like one continuous experience, not separate blocks stacked on top of each other.
- Fast loading despite animations — use CSS animations and intersection observers, not heavy libraries.
- The site itself is the pitch. If someone lands on this page and thinks “I want my website to feel like THIS” — you’ve already won.

## ANIMATION GUIDELINES

The animations are critical — they’re what makes this site feel premium and different from every other agency page.

- **Scroll-triggered reveals:** Elements fade in and slide up slightly (translateY 20-30px to 0) as they enter the viewport. Use intersection observer, not scroll position.
- **Staggered entrances:** When multiple elements appear together (like the feature grid or comparison columns), stagger them with 100-150ms delays so they cascade in rather than appearing all at once.
- **Headline animations:** Large headlines can use a clip/reveal animation — text slides in from behind a mask, or characters fade in sequentially. Keep it subtle and fast (400-600ms).
- **Smooth scrolling:** When CTA buttons scroll to the sentence builder, use smooth scroll behavior (800-1000ms ease-out).
- **Hover states:** Buttons and cards should have subtle hover transitions — slight scale, color shift, or shadow change. 200ms ease.
- **Parallax (subtle):** Section backgrounds or decorative elements can move at slightly different scroll speeds for depth. Very subtle — 5-10% parallax, not dramatic.
- **No bounce, no wobble, no playfulness.** Every animation should feel like it has weight and intention. Think: a heavy glass door closing smoothly, not a rubber ball bouncing.

All animations should be CSS-based or use lightweight intersection observer JS. No heavy animation libraries. Performance matters — the site should feel fast even with all the motion.
