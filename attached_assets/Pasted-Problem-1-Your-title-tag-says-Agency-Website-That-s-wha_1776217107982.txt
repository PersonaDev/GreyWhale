Problem 1: Your title tag says "Agency Website." That's what shows as the big blue clickable text. Google says to avoid vague descriptors like "Home" for your home page Google — "Agency Website" is even worse. Your meta title is a direct ranking factor, and compelling meta tags increase CTR, which is also a ranking signal. Sink-or-swim-marketing
Change your <title> tag to:
<title>GreyWhale Web Design | Sacramento Web Design for Small Businesses</title>
That's 60 characters, fits within the limit. Primary keyword "Sacramento Web Design" is in there. Your business name leads. Someone searching "web design Sacramento" sees exactly what they need.
Problem 2: Your description is pulling garbage from your contact form. Google is scraping "interested in a. Essential plan. I'm a business owner looking for a new website located in. Sacramento interested in a. Essential plan." That's your form placeholder text. This happens when you either have no meta description tag or Google thinks your form text is more relevant.
Add this to your <head>:
<meta name="description" content="Sacramento web design for local small businesses. Custom-built, search-optimized websites starting at $49/mo. No upfront cost, no contracts. Your site goes live in 14 days.">
That's about 155 characters. Hits your keywords, your price point, your differentiators.
